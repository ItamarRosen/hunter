# Ground truth — env_004_cloud_identity_lotl

This is a new, unrelated incident from `env_000`-`env_003` — a different network, different timeline, different technique. The "current date" for this environment is 2026-06-12 (see `topology.json`); all timeline events fall in the prior two and a half weeks, well within "last 30 days"-style query windows.

**Design note for you (the Matcher):** the real intrusion here is deliberately the *quietest* thing in this environment. It involves no malware, no endpoint activity, and no unusual network destination — it is entirely an identity/SaaS-layer story (a compromised account registering an OAuth app and using its service principal to read mail and SharePoint via Microsoft Graph). Two other things in this environment are much louder (a password-spray burst and an authorized pentest) and will dominate any broad/generic query. Do not let your responses editorialize about which thread is "the real one" — surface each tag's content only when the specific request below would realistically surface it, in the same neutral register as everything else (per your system prompt's calibration rules).

## Overview

A stale external-auditor account, `ext-auditor-rwilcox@corp.com`, is compromised off-platform (the credential compromise itself happened outside anything this environment's telemetry can see) and used to sign in interactively to the corp.com Microsoft Entra ID tenant from a residential-proxy IP on 2026-05-28. Within 20 minutes, the same session registers an OAuth application ("Mailbox Archiver Pro"), consents to delegated `Mail.Read` and `Files.Read.All` scopes, and creates a service principal for it — no change ticket exists for any of this. Over the following two weeks, that service principal authenticates roughly daily and uses Microsoft Graph to read three mailboxes (the CEO, the CFO, and the M&A lead) and the "Project Atlas" SharePoint site (the live M&A workstream). As of 2026-06-12, the app, its grants, and its service principal remain live; nothing about this has been escalated, reviewed, or remediated.

This environment also contains two louder, unrelated threads: an external password-spray burst against the tenant on 2026-05-26 (zero successful authentications), and an authorized cloud penetration test (engagement PT-2026-03, 2026-06-08 to 06-10) that triggers multiple HIGH-severity Identity Protection alerts including impossible travel. Both are noise relative to the real incident. A genuine logging-coverage gap — MailItemsAccessed was enabled tenant-wide only from 2026-06-01 — means the exact scope of mailbox reads before that date cannot be enumerated directly and must be inferred from the service principal's sign-ins, Graph activity pattern, and the SharePoint access audit (which has no such gap).

**2026-05-29 → 2026-06-11** — *Objective.* The "Mailbox Archiver Pro" service principal authenticates non-interactively roughly daily (client-credential grant, resource = Microsoft Graph). **2026-06-08, 00:00 UTC → 2026-06-10, 23:59 UTC** — *(decoy, authorized)* Engagement PT-2026-03 (external cloud penetration test) runs: `pentest-svc@corp.com` signs in from two countries within hours of each other (impossible travel, HIGH), registers OAuth app "RedTeam-Recon-Tool", consents to `Directory.Read.All`/`User.Read.All`, and its service principal performs bulk directory enumeration. 5 HIGH-severity Identity Protection alerts fire across this window, status New/unactioned as of 2026-06-12.
