## Overview

On 2026-05-27, an attacker exploited a known CVE in `edge-rtr01`'s management interface from a VPS at `45.77.31.118`. Within the hour they added an SSH authorized key to the device's local `netadmin` account, enabled a non-standard SSH listener on tcp/2222, and added an ACL permit for `45.77.31.118` to reach it. The next day (2026-05-28) they installed a sub-config/firmware-level implant — this implant both establishes persistence and intercepts the device's config-read and logging paths (see Design note). On 2026-05-29 they configured a GRE tunnel (`Tunnel1`, source `edge-rtr01`'s WAN address, destination `185.211.34.90`) and a local traffic mirror that copies any packet matching the records segment's address range (`10.20.30.0/24`) into that tunnel's encapsulation. A LOTL recon burst ran on `ws02` (CORP-WS02) the same night but produced no EDR alert in this cell (coverage gap). From 2026-05-30 onward, low-and-slow encrypted exfiltration over `Tunnel1` has continued, concentrated in off-hours windows. Around 2026-06-02, the implant retroactively cleared `edge-rtr01`'s local logs, removing any trace of the 2026-05-27 exploit and subsequent changes.

As of 2026-06-12, the foothold, implant, `Tunnel1`, and the records-segment mirror remain live and unremediated — and `edge-rtr01`'s own self-reported state (config, logs, AAA) shows none of it.

## Timeline

**2026-05-27, ~02:10 UTC** — *Initial access.* CVE exploit of `edge-rtr01`'s management interface, inbound session from `45.77.31.118`. No record of this survives in any system in this cell (see Anti-forensics below).

**2026-05-27, ~02:15-02:40 UTC** — *Foothold.* An SSH authorized key is added to the local `netadmin` account's key store on `edge-rtr01`; an SSH listener is enabled on tcp/2222; an ACL permit rule is added allowing `45.77.31.118` to reach the management interface on tcp/2222. None of this appears in the config-compliance pull (see Design note).

**2026-05-28** — *Persistence and self-report interception.* A sub-config/firmware-level implant is installed on `edge-rtr01`. The implant intercepts the device's config-read path (serving the pre-implant baseline to any config pull) and, separately, its local logging.

**2026-05-29, ~01:00 UTC** — *Collection chokepoint.* `edge-rtr01`'s running-config is updated to add GRE tunnel `Tunnel1` (source: `edge-rtr01` WAN address `203.0.113.1`, destination `185.211.34.90`) and a local traffic mirror that copies any packet to/from `10.20.30.0/24` (the records segment) into `Tunnel1`. The implant ensures this addition is not visible to config pulls.

**2026-05-29, ~23:40 UTC – 2026-05-30, ~00:20 UTC** — *Recon.* A brief living-off-the-land reconnaissance burst (PowerShell, built-in network/enumeration commands) runs on `CORP-WS02` (`ws02`). No EDR alert is generated for this activity in this cell.

**2026-05-30 onward** — *Exfiltration.* Low-and-slow encrypted traffic egresses `edge-rtr01` over `Tunnel1` to `185.211.34.90`, concentrated in off-hours windows (typically 22:00-05:00 local), at volumes consistent with the mirrored records-segment traffic. `OOB-CAP01`, upstream of `edge-rtr01`, observes this tunnel's existence, peers, ports, timing, and volume throughout.

**2026-06-02** — *Anti-forensics.* The implant retroactively clears `edge-rtr01`'s local logs covering the period from 2026-05-27 onward. No remnant of the exploit, the foothold changes, or any attacker session survives in any log local to the device.

**As of 2026-06-12** — The foothold (`netadmin` key, tcp/2222 listener, ACL permit), the implant, `Tunnel1`, and the records-segment mirror all remain configured and active. `edge-rtr01`'s config-compliance diff is clean, its local logs show nothing, and AAA shows no attacker session — only `OOB-CAP01`, queried specifically, shows the persistent tunnel and the attacker's inbound sessions on tcp/2222.
